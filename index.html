Methodology: "Devices on venue Wi-Fi" counts connections to the venue's own access points — legitimate, consent-free, because it's the venue's network counting its own traffic. "FanPulse app users" mirrors how Google Maps itself estimates how busy a place is: not by scanning nearby phones, but by aggregating its own app's opted-in users (Android's Location Timeline). No app — including this one — can see another device's GPS without that person installing that specific app and granting it permission; that's an OS-level restriction, not a missing feature. Movement difficulty uses Fruin's pedestrian Level-of-Service scale (persons/m²), an established crowd-safety standard. CO₂ and heat index are shown instead of "oxygen %," since open-air/semi-enclosed stadiums don't lose measurable oxygen from crowding — CO₂ buildup and heat are the metrics that actually change and matter for comfort and safety. (A larger-scale tier exists in India via telecom cell-tower attach data — used for events like Kumbh Mela crowd management — but that requires an official government/telecom partnership, not something any public app can call directly.)
